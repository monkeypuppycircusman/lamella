Lamella — the Latin name for the vibrating reed tongue.
The background is a dark ancient room with dust motes drifting in no particular direction, and a faint warm orange-red glow that breathes slowly from the center — the body heat of the cavity. The cursor is a plucking finger with three fingertip segments and an orange-red dot at the tip.

The SVG is a jaw harp held between two rows of barely-visible teeth. The upper jaw has individual tooth shapes. The lower jaw has its own set. Between them the instrument sits — a horseshoe iron frame with two vertical prongs, a long handle extending right with leather grip-wrap lines and a highlight along the top edge, rivet heads at the prong joints, a ball-joint at the bottom bight. The reed runs down the center of the frame, four pixels wide with an iron gradient — brighter at center, dark at edges — with a highlight line and a rounded tip at the bottom. When you pluck it the reed animates a realistic wobble: deflects right, springs back left, damps to center. When sustained it shimmers continuously. A mouth-cavity glow ellipse sits behind the frame and heats to orange-red when playing. Formant waves expand outward from the cavity — two different sized ellipses, one for each formant, in different colors.

The physics is exactly right. A jaw harp reed is clamped at one end, free at the other. It vibrates at its natural frequency — you cannot change that note. What you can change is which harmonic the mouth cavity amplifies. The mouth acts as a dual-bandpass filter:
F1 (jaw height/openness) is on the X axis of the XY pad: slide left = jaw open = higher F1 = AH sound (~800Hz). Slide right = jaw closed = lower F1 = OO sound (~300Hz).
F2 (tongue position front-to-back) is on the Y axis: bottom = tongue pulled back = low F2 = throat resonance (~870Hz). Top = tongue forward = high F2 = nasal/EE resonance (~2300Hz).
The display panel on the right shows the current vowel name and the exact F1/F2 frequencies in real time as you drag. When you move the XY pad while notes are playing, the bandpass filters update in real time via setTargetAtTime so the timbre morphs continuously without clicks. This is live formant control — the same thing your mouth does when you speak, but mapped to a touchpad.
Four reed materials change the spectral character: Forged Iron (sawtooth, full harmonics), Bone Reed (triangle, warmer, faster decay), Bamboo (brighter shift, quickest decay), Brass (slightly sharper, longest ring).
Eight pads:

Single Pluck — one transient, natural decay
Reed Roll — 8 rapid plucks, the jaw harp tremolo technique
Throat Drone (hold) — continuous buzz with cavity shaping
Overtone Climb — moves the XY through 6 positions while plucking at each, climbing the harmonic series as the cavity shape changes
Vowel Sweep — the XY moves in a slow circular pattern automatically while the reed holds, the vowel morphing continuously — this is the classic jaw harp technique
Tremolo — 13 fast plucks over 0.6 seconds
Nasal Cavity (hold) — locks XY to high F2 position (tongue forward), emphasizing the nasal formant at 2300Hz
Ritual Pattern — 12 plucks with slowly rotating XY between each, a pattern that spans the whole formant space
